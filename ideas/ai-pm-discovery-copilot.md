# AI-PM Discovery Copilot (Cursor for Product Managers)

**Date:** 2026-03-28
**Source:** Y Combinator Requests for Startups 2025
**Status:** Signal but no money

---

## Problem Statement

- **Who:** Product managers at seed-to-Series B startups running customer discovery in parallel with shipping
- **What:** No integrated tool connects the full discovery loop — customer interviews, usage data, support tickets, and feature requests — into a structured "what to build next" output. PMs synthesize this manually across Notion, Figma, Jira, and spreadsheets.
- **When:** Every sprint planning and roadmap cycle; acutely during quarterly planning

---

| Source | Quote or Evidence | Intensity |

| ChatPRD user (Series B lead) | "The quality of our specs has gone up dramatically." | Medium |
| YC RFS 2025 | "No integrated AI-native tool for the full discovery loop: customer interviews, usage data synthesis, feature definition. Current PM outputs (PRDs, Figma mocks, Jira tickets) are designed for human engineers, not AI agents." | High |
| PM community (aggregated) | "Decision-making relies on guesswork rather than data"; "Feedback collection and analysis consume excessive time"; "Cross-functional team alignment remains challenging" | Medium |

**Workarounds people use today:**
- Notion + manual tagging of interview transcripts
- ChatGPT for drafting PRDs (no context persistence)
- Productboard or Canny for feedback, with manual synthesis
- Spreadsheets to track themes across customer conversations

## Shab Fit

**Verdict:** Weak fit

- Shab's profile matches perfectly (bridges tech/business, 0-to-1 thinking, PM fluency). But this is a globally saturated market: ChatPRD has 100k users, Productboard is well-funded, Mistly just launched on PH. YC calling it out as a gap suggests the specific *full discovery loop* angle is still open — but differentiation requires a very precise positioning (e.g., India-focused PM teams, or "discovery OS for AI-native startups") and sustained execution against funded players. Main risk: Shab's innovation bias will attract him to the clever version of this idea, not the distribution-first version.

---

## Recommended Next Step

Before building anything, validate the specific gap: interview 10 PMs who use ChatPRD + Productboard and ask what they still do manually. If the answer clusters around "interview synthesis → spec," there may be a wedge. Otherwise, this space requires a distribution moat, not a better product.
