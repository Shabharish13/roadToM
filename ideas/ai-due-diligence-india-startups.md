# AI Startup Due Diligence Assistant (India Ecosystem)

**Date:** 2026-03-28
**Source:** NeedGap ("due diligence is too time-consuming and expensive") + HN + X/Twitter
**Status:** Saturated

---

## Problem Statement

- **Who:** Angel investors, micro-VCs, and family offices doing early-stage deals in India
- **What:** Standard startup DD takes 6–8 weeks and costs $25k–$500k via consultants — out of reach for small check writers who need to move fast in a competitive deal market
- **When:** Pre-term sheet and post-term-sheet stages; India-specific complexity (MCA filings, cap table hygiene, GST compliance) adds extra burden

**Workarounds people use today:**
- Manual MCA search + LinkedIn + news scraping
- Hiring CA firms for financial DD
- Peer-to-peer reference checks in investor WhatsApp groups
- Skipping formal DD entirely for small cheques (high risk)

## Shab Fit

**Verdict:** Hard pass

- The problem is clear and the India-specific angle (MCA data, GSTIN verification, PAN linkage, founder background checks) is genuinely underserved by global tools. BUT: the primary buyers are institutional investors and family offices — the hard part is enterprise/institutional sales, relationship trust, and regulatory accuracy. DiligenceSquared just raised $5M from YC. Wokelo, Clarum, SignalX are all funded and moving fast. The moat here is data relationships and legal accuracy, not product thinking. This fails Shab's filter on enterprise sales complexity and elite technical depth in a regulated domain.

---

## Recommended Next Step

Pass. If this space is interesting to Shab as a problem, consider a much narrower wedge: a public India startup data aggregator (MCA + Tofler + news + Crunchbase) as a free tool to build distribution first, then layer paid features. But don't start with the enterprise buyer.
